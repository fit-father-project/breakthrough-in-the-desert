Strategic rationale

Bronze reads as carved, ceremonial, and old — the same register the brand thesis wants. Heavy drop shadow + inner highlight gives real depth so the button looks pressed into the page rather than painted on. Subtle SVG noise overlay adds a tactile aged-metal feel. The text is embossed (1px shadow) for that "stamped into metal" effect. Hover deepens the warm halo around it.

Visual spec

BG: linear-gradient 135deg #a87a48 → #8c5e30 → #6e4a26 (aged bronze)
Inner highlight: top 1px rgba(255,230,180,.5), bottom 1px rgba(0,0,0,.45)
Shadow: 0 14px 36px rgba(80,50,20,.5) + 0 0 48px rgba(168,122,72,.25)
Texture: SVG fractal noise overlay at 18% opacity, blend-mode overlay
Text: Schibsted Grotesk 700, 0.24em tracking, embossed shadow
Hover: lift 2px + intensified gold halo + arrow nudge

Strategic rationale

The button is a sheet of frosted glass floating over the photo, with a soft warm halo pulsing behind it. Lets the imagery breathe through the button rather than blocking it. Modern, premium, and the slow pulsing halo creates a low-key "this is alive" cue without being needy. The arrow ring inverts on hover from gold-on-glass to ink-on-gold — a quiet but distinctive flip.

Visual spec

BG: rgba(255,255,255,.08) + backdrop-filter blur(16px) saturate(140%)
Border: 1px solid rgba(255,255,255,.32)
Halo: radial gradient warm gold behind button, animates 4s pulse
Inner highlight: top 1px rgba(255,255,255,.25)
Arrow: circular gold-outlined ring; inverts to filled gold on hover
Hover: bg lifts to .14 opacity, border to gold, arrow filled, ink-color arrow icon

Strategic rationale

The most restrained option. Just a thin gold outline with a faint second outline offset 6px outward — the "carved" doubled border, like a letterpress card or museum signage. The text and arrow sit in gold inside transparent space. On hover the inner rectangle fills ember from left to right and the text flips to white. The most editorial choice — quiet at rest, dramatic on interaction.

Visual spec

BG: transparent at rest, fills ember on hover from left
Border: 1px solid gold (inner), 1px solid gold at 32% opacity offset -6px (outer)
Text: Schibsted Grotesk 600, 0.3em tracking, gold at rest / white on hover
Arrow: long thin chevron, matches text color
Hover: ember sweep fills L→R over 500ms, outer rule collapses to inner, text turns white, arrow shifts right 4px

The call

If it were my pick.

Direction A · Etched Bronze. It carries the most brand weight — bronze and gold are the brand's metallic register, the carved tactile feel matches the "sacred" framing, and the embossed text reads as a thing that was stamped into existence rather than designed yesterday. Frosted Halo is the close runner-up if you want a more contemporary "Apple-keynote" register. Carved Outline is the move only if the page leans hard editorial and the button needs to disappear at rest.
