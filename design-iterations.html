Breakthrough in the Desert
five sections, fifteen directions.

An audit and design exploration of the event landing page. The copy is locked and strong. This document pushes the visual execution to match the ambition of the writing. Each of the five highest-leverage sections is explored through three genuinely distinct directions, rendered as real previews, scored by how hard each one converts.

Functional goal

Move a 40+ reader from quiet stagnation to a booked seat. Every section either builds belief or removes friction. Nothing is decoration.

Emotional register

Soul-aware but grounded. The page should feel like a confessional that becomes an invitation. Premium, never hype. It must feel worth the price before the price is named.

Where it falls short today

Faculty has zero faces. Emoji icons undercut the craft. Hero is quiet rather than arresting. Venue is a placeholder. Real proof photos are underused.

The bar

It should look unmistakably premium — the kind of page where the design itself is evidence the room will be worth three days of your life.

Design direction

Custom thin-stroke line icons inside hairline circles, in rust. Each column carries a small numeral and is separated by a 1px rule rather than floating in space. The grid stops looking like a feature block and starts looking like a page from a well-set book.

Strategic rationale

Emoji are operating-system artifacts — they render differently on every device and read as casual. Bespoke iconography is a craft signal that quietly says "everything here was considered." Numbering implies a sequence, a journey, which is exactly the brand promise. Lowest risk, highest fit with the existing system.

Visual spec

Icons: 1.4px stroke SVG, 42px circle, rust #b85c3a.   Type: Fraunces 600 word, Inter 400 body, Inter 700 numeral in gold.   Layout: 3-col grid, 1px vertical dividers, left-aligned text within each cell.   Motion: icons draw-on via stroke-dashoffset when section enters view.

The trade-off worth your attention.

The one genuine fork is the Hero. The Threshold (recommended) keeps the event bigger than any one person and is the safer, more scalable choice. The Witness is more emotionally magnetic and uses a real, striking founder portrait — but it spends the founder card early and overlaps the founder section. If Breakthrough markets primarily on Dr. Anthony's personal brand, The Witness is worth a real look. If it markets as a collective experience, The Threshold wins.
