A Letter From the Founder

I created this event for the
version of me I want to be at ninety.

I'm Dr. Anthony Balduzzi, founder of Fit Father & Fit Mother. Over the past decade I've helped 70,000+ families reclaim their health and vitality. I know how present choices shape our future.

When I imagine myself at age 90, my body still has muscle and strength. I'm exercising in the park with my daughter Brooke and grandkids by my side. My mind is sharp. I'm still reading, enjoying deep discussions, exploring the mysteries of the universe. My heart is happy and grateful, reflecting on a life of meaningful work, friendships, growth, and service.

Now imagine the best version of YOU at age 90.

You're also fit and strong, playing with your great-grandkids. You're surrounded by the people you love most. Feeling grateful for all you experienced, in every chapter.

You're genuinely proud because there was another version of you where you didn't live fully. Where you allowed your health to slip. Where relationships crumbled. Where you looked back with regret.

We don't end up thriving in old age by accident. We build it intentionally. The next ten years lay the foundation. So, are you on track?

Is your physical health dialed in? Are your relationships connected? Are you still learning & growing? Are you on track to thrive into old age?

If you're like me, you're doing good. Yet you have gaps. Bad habits to drop. Areas of life to deepen for greater wellbeing. More memories to make.

This is why I created Breakthrough in the Desert. For 3 days we come together to look honestly at life: what's working and what's not. Together, we'll improve our health, our relationships, our growth. We will connect, laugh, play, and walk through real transformation with a circle of like-minded people who want to thrive for life.

Are you ready to invest in your ideal future? If yes, I'd love to have you with me in the room this August at Breakthrough in the Desert.

— Dr. Anthony Balduzzi

The Venue

Where breakthrough happens.

Arizona Grand Resort · Phoenix, Arizona

The desert has been a place of transformation for as long as humans have existed. Jesus. Moses. The Buddha. Indigenous vision quests. Sufi mystics. There's a reason the great traditions all sent their seekers into the desert when something inside them needed to break open.

You'll spend three days in that same landscape — not roughing it, but rooted in it. The Arizona Grand Resort sits against South Mountain, the largest municipal park in the country, with miles of sunrise hiking trails just steps from the door. The venue is 10 minutes from Phoenix Sky Harbor and a short drive from Sedona and the red rock country if you want to extend your trip.

This is your wellness vacation, your retreat, and your breakthrough — all in one place.

About the desert in August

Yes, it's hot. It's also dry — which most people find dramatically more comfortable than the humidity they're used to. Outside of optional sunrise hikes (stunning at 6am when the desert is cool), every minute of the event experience happens inside an air-conditioned venue. And when you're not in session, the 7-acre water park and lazy river make 105° feel like a gift, not a problem.

The desert in August is part of the magic. Embrace it.
