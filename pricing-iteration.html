What's actually different

Six concrete changes from the v2 pricing section, each chosen to move a specific lever for the 40+ buyer who's asking "is this worth it?"

  • The reframe lands first. "This isn't an expense. It's the gift you give your next chapter." sits in italic Newsreader directly above the cards — primes the emotional state before any dollar sign appears.
  • VIP visibly elevated. 18% wider, lifted on real shadow (0 36px 72px ink + 0 0 100px gold halo), gold accent typography, ribbon. The eye lands on it first by intention.
  • Payment plan surfaced. "or 3 payments of $432" sits directly under the price in Schibsted Grotesk, not buried as fine print. Many over-40 buyers will read the monthly number before the total.
  • Partner add-on inline. Instead of a sub-card, a tinted inline strip with a ledger-style "+" mark. Reads as an optional add, not a separate decision.
  • Trust line kills three objections in one band. "Transferable through July 1 · 3-pay at checkout · Non-refundable" — dots-separated row directly under the cards. Anticipates the "what if I can't go" friction.
  • Closing emotional line. "Bring your partner. Breakthroughs are deeper when shared." Replaces the longer paragraph in v2. One italic Newsreader line, anchors the soul of the sell.

Mobile-specific moves

  • VIP card stacks first on mobile. Buyer sees the recommended option immediately, scrolls past for the lighter option.
  • Touch targets 54-58px tall. Comfortable for thumbs at any age.
  • Pricing typography boosted — body copy 0.82rem on mobile (was 0.76 in v2), partner add-on lighter padding to fit phone width without crowding.
  • Trust line stacks vertically on phone so each item gets its own beat. No squinting at a row of small text.
